Description¶

The dataset we will be working with is ‘Current Employee Names, Salaries, and Position Titles’ in the city of Chicago (https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/current-employee-names-salaries-and-position-titles). The goals for our project are to answer multiple questions with this data, focusing on the salary variability within and across different departments. We want to investigate if salary is greater within or across each department, if there are similar/same job titles in different departments that aren’t paid equally, what the range/mean/median of salaries are across each department, and how spread out the salaries are. We will graph our results and state concrete conclusions.

Three interesting statistics¶

The first interesting statistic from the first 49 rows of the dataset are that 40% of workers are paid hourly, while the rest are annual. Second, 48 out of 49 employees are full time. Lastly, the highest earning employee is a Managing Deputy Commissioner in the Department of Fleet and Facility Management, with an annual salary of $194,688.

Convert Hourly Wages to Annual Salary¶

Some employees are paid hourly. In order to make them comparable across employees, we estimate the annual salary using the equation:

Annual salary = Hourly Rate x Typical Hours x 52 Weeks

Once this is done, we further clean the data by dropping the typical hours column, as it will no longer be used.

In [ ]:
#Convery hourly to annual salary using above equation
current.loc[
    (current['Salary or Hourly'] == 'HOURLY') & (current['Annual Salary'].isna()),
    'Annual Salary'
] = current['Hourly Rate'] * current['Typical Hours'] * 52

#Drop Typical Hours column
current = current.drop(columns=['Typical Hours'] , errors='ignore')

Analysis of Salary Statistics by Department¶

The summary statistics above provide an insight into how salaries vary across Chicago City departments. Knowing the mean and median salaries help us to understand the typical pay level in each department, and the standard deviation measures how spread out the salaries are in that department.

For example, the Chicago Fire Department and Chicago Police Department have higher mean salaries when compared to other departments, likely reflecting specialized training or experience requirements.

The standard deviation show that some departments have wider ranges of salaries. For example, the Chicago Department of Aviation has a large standard deviation as well as a high maximum slaary, showing a large range of salary.

These statistics are relevant to our research questions because they help us analyze whether salary variability occurs within departments or across departments. By comparing these summary statistics, we can start to see how salary levels and salary spread differ depending on the department.

Average Salary by Department¶

To better understand this overall distrubution, we create a bar chart of the average annual salaries for each department. This will allow us to compare salary levels between departments and identify departments with higher average pay. This addresses our research question about salary variability across departments.

In [ ]:
# Calculate average salary for each department
avg_salary_dept = current.groupby('Department')['Annual Salary'].mean()

# Select the top 10 departments with the highest average salaries
top10 = avg_salary_dept.sort_values(ascending=False).head(10)

# Plot the results
plt.figure(figsize=(10,6))
top10.plot(kind='bar')

plt.title("Average Salary by Top 10 Department")
plt.ylabel("Average Annual Salary")
plt.xlabel("Department")
plt.xticks(rotation=45)

plt.show()

Analysis of Average Salary by Top 10 Department¶

The bar chart highlights clear differences in the average salaries. The Board of Ethics has the highest average salary.

These results reflect specialization within departments, showing that departments like the Board of Ethics, and Department of Buildings likely have more specialized positions that may require more training or more complex seniority levels.
